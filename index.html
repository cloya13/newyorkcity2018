New York 2018

Aubrey & Christian

Day 1 Day 2 Day 3 Day 4 Day 5 Day 6 Day 7

After an 11 hour flight delay, we FINALLY made it to NYC! Christian had a hot dog from a stand on 53rd and we wandered around Midtown for about an hour before catching some Zzzz's.

From an early morning walk through central park, to a trip down 5th ave; Day two was definitely one of our favorites! St. Patrick's Cathedral was absolutley remarkable. Times Square on the other hand, was too much for Chris & I. It didn't take long for us to realize that we wanted to stay far away from that area... We finished off our night with the Broadway show "Wicked". If you plan on going to New York, this play is a MUST!

YAY for our first subway ride! We caught a train headed downtown-last stop... One World Trade Center.The 9/11 museum and memorial were truly life changing. We were only five years old when the attacks took place, so to finally come face to face with history brought out a multitude of overwhelming emotions. To all the people who lost their lives at work that day, To all of the first responders who risked their lives for others safety, To the nurses who were there to clean the wounds-- we love you, we thank you, and we will never forget you.

Shopping, shopping, and more shopping!! We took the subway to the Bronx and had the pleasure of attending the 2018 Subway Series!! #bronxbombers #yankeestadium #allrise

New york is known for its amazing street art. My personal favorite? These inflatable flowers that can be found in various places around Manhattan right now! "[flowers] have never been able to compete with the scale of their surroundings- until now." We explored the American Museum of Natural History which gave us total "Night at the Museum" Ben Stiller Vibes.

Brooklyn bridge, wall street, and subway mix-ups oh my! We somehow ended up in Brooklyn in our attempt to head back uptown. Whoopsie! We had mastered the subway system.. until now. Sometimes mess ups lead to awesome adventures, and for that we're thankful! The weather this week was gorgeous. It was 75 and sunny everyday until Sunday and even then, it was beautiful. Chris and I were like little kids running around NYC in the rain.

Just look at The New York Public Library. Isn't it lovely?! My teacher heart is swooning. We spent the evening wandering around southern New York with our friend Gracie who works for a designer here. She took us to &pizza- a build-your-own, affordable, delicious pizzeria. After dinner, she showed us Washington Square Park where we saw street performers, caught up on the past two years of life, and thats when she asked us.. "What is it like being in love?" and Christian replied, "Its like you don't want to spend time with anyone else- you only want to be with that person." And to that I say Cheers! To friendships, to love, to NYC 2018, and to our future.
